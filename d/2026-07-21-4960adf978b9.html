Politico NatSec Daily · Jul 20

Iran hedges even as it escalates, pursuing a strategy of limited pressure

Ten days into consecutive U.S. strikes, Iran is concentrating retaliation on Jordan, Bahrain and Kuwait — states that host major U.S. installations but lack the economic weight of Saudi Arabia or the UAE, which Tehran has hit less often. Analysts read this as a deliberate effort to raise the cost of the U.S. regional presence and split the Gulf without provoking a unified coalition or all-out war. Former officials warn the calculus flips if the U.S. begins striking significant Iranian energy infrastructure.

So what — Tehran is managing escalation to pressure host nations, not to trigger a general war; commanders should expect continued harassment of bases in Jordan/Bahrain/Kuwait and plan force protection and dispersal accordingly, while watching U.S. energy-infrastructure strikes as the likely trip-wire for a wider fight.
