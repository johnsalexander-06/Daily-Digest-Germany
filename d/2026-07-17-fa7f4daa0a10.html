Microsoft's Secure Boot has been broken for a decade and nobody noticed

ESET researchers found 11 old UEFI shims — some dating to 2013 — that Microsoft never revoked, letting attackers bypass Secure Boot on both Windows and Linux using signed but vulnerable boot components. The consequence is persistent bootkits that survive a full OS reinstall, sitting below anything an endpoint agent can see. Only recent revocation updates and proper key management close these specific gaps.

So WhatReimaging a compromised machine may not clear the compromise, which invalidates the standard incident-response playbook most units use; ask your cyber defenders whether the revocation updates are actually deployed across your fleet, not merely available.

📅 On This Day in History

17 July 1944 — Port Chicago. An ammunition explosion at the Port Chicago Naval Magazine in California killed 320 sailors and civilians, most of them African American enlisted men loading munitions under segregated conditions. The subsequent refusal of 50 survivors to return to the same unsafe work — and their mutiny convictions — became a catalyst for the desegregation of the US Navy.
17 July 1945 — Potsdam opens. Truman, Churchill (later Attlee), and Stalin convened outside Berlin to settle the shape of postwar Europe. Truman arrived having just learned that the Trinity test had succeeded the day before — a fact that shadowed every conversation at the table.
